First Information Report (FIR) Guide

Understanding the FIR Filing Process

Understanding Cognizable Offenses

Cognizable offenses are serious crimes where police can register a case, investigate, and make arrests without prior magistrate approval.

Who Can File an FIR?

  • The victim
  • Witnesses to the crime
  • Any person with knowledge of the offense

Where to File?

  • Local police station in crime jurisdiction
  • Online through state police portals
  • Senior police officials in special cases

Required Information

  • Details of the offense (time, date, location)
  • Names/descriptions of accused (if known)
  • Witness information (if available)
  • Supporting evidence or documents

    • FIR Filing Process

    1. Visit the police station in jurisdiction
    2. Provide detailed information about the incident
    3. Review and verify the recorded information
    4. Sign the FIR document
    5. Collect your free copy of the FIR

    If Police Refuse to File FIR

    Alternative Actions:

    • Contact Senior Police Officials (SP/Commissioner)
    • Approach the Magistrate under Section 156(3) CrPC
    • Use online grievance portals
    • Contact National Human Rights Commission
